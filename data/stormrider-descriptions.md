## REGION 1 — SOUTHERN NICARAGUA (12 spots)

### 1. Popoyo
**URL :** https://stormrider.surf/break/popoyo

**The Surf.** The peak at Popoyo displays alluring symmetry over the flattish reef and sand slab at the southern end of Playa Guasacate. Peels off predictably at a nice pace for vertical whacks and the odd crumbly cover-up. Better at low incoming tide, the peak will hold up to double overhead with longer barrel sections before closing-out and a considerable crowd as well. The Outer Reef (400m offshore) is only for the very bravest big wave riders — the lefts look deceptively perfect from distance but feature shallow rocks, boils and riverine currents sculpting heaving, bloodthirsty barrels with a do-or-die end section. Easily handles 20ft faces and it gets scarier by the minute on the drop.

**The Environment.** One of the most consistent and crowded waves in the country, but worth the effort when it is firing. Park at south end of town (leave nothing of value in the car) and cross the river to get to the break.

### 2. Colorado
**URL :** https://stormrider.surf/break/colorado

**The Surf.** The rivermouth sandbars at Playa Colorado are quite possibly the hollowest, fastest barrels in the country when a SW or even better a W swell arrives. Quite sensitive to tide and wind, favouring rights and the surfers staying in the private Hacienda Iguana. Tube junkies paradise when it comes together, which isn't always.

**The Environment.** Crowds arrive by boat to join the residents who need to avoid the copious stingrays on the shuffle out.

### 3. Panga Drops
**URL :** https://stormrider.surf/break/panga-drops

**The Surf.** Bombora-style horseshoe-shaped reef picks up everything going and offers whizzing walls, inside barrels and fading shoulders that shift all over the large playing field. Super-fun for mere mortals on both short and longboards. Gets easily ruffled by the offshores, being so far out, so look for glassy conditions.

**The Environment.** It's sometimes crowded, but the waves scatter the crew quickly. Walk from Gigante or boat in to avoid the long paddle.

### 4. Playa Santana
**URL :** https://stormrider.surf/break/playa-santana

**The Surf.** Playa Santana has heavy beachbreak barrels that are highly sought after and always crowded on higher tides. Peaky and always powerful, even on small swells, then the barrels get throaty as the swell increases. Skills required to handle the drops and hustling.

**The Environment.** Only surfable by boat unless you are staying in the Rancho Santana private estate, or the Malibu Popoyo, or are one of the locals from nearby Limon town.

### 5. Manzanillo
**URL :** https://stormrider.surf/break/manzanillo

**The Surf.** One of the first waves to receive any media attention in the '90s and when the specific SSW swell wraps in, it is one of Nicaragua's best draw-cards. A wedgy, cliff-bounce take-off leads into a series of walled sections, with occasional tubes, before racing into the beachbreak on the inside. A longish ride from tip to tail and definitely best around mid tide on moderate swells below 225º.

**The Environment.** Impressive cliff backdrop and only accessible by panga unless staying in the Mukul 5-star resort. Often crowded because it appeals to the average surfer, but the long paddle back out and various sections can spread the bodies. Beware of rocks and urchins.

### 6. Playa Rosada
**URL :** https://stormrider.surf/break/playa-rosada

**The Surf.** Straight-shooting, short, speedy lefts over a carpet of craggy, urchin-sprinkled reef right in front of the Rancho Santana gated community. It's shallow but high tide can be more dangerous as it hides a few critical rocks in the line-up.

**The Environment.** Access by boat or walking round the rocks from Playa Santana. There's no safe access from the rocks so paddle from the beach if coming from land. Rip runs N in big swells.

### 7. Playa Maderas
**URL :** https://stormrider.surf/break/playa-maderas

**The Surf.** The beachbreak of Playa Maderas is probably the best around San Juan. Very consistent, sometimes hollow, it gets challenging in overhead swells. Often crowded with all types of surfer and surfcraft. The adjacent beaches of Majagual and Marsella can have lower quality waves with less people.

**The Environment.** Hosted the first national contest in May 2002. The fishing village of San Juan del Sur has become a cruise port and seaside resort, popular with Nicaraguan teenagers as much as gringo surfers. The surf isn't very good within the city's horseshoe-shaped bay, but many spots are accessible from here.

### 8. El Remanso
**URL :** https://stormrider.surf/break/el-remanso

**The Surf.** San Juan del Sur has become a cruise port and seaside resort, popular with Nicaraguan teenagers as much as gringo surfers. The surf isn't very good within the city's horseshoe-shaped bay, so some locals go south to surf second-rate beachbreaks at the picturesque cove of El Remanso.

**The Environment.** Won't handle too much swell but some fun waves particularly for beginners and improvers.

### 9. Tamarind
**URL :** https://stormrider.surf/break/tamarind

**The Surf.** Better sessions should be found at the next beach, Playa Tamarind, where quick barrel opportunities occur on clean small swells, although on most days, the right pointbreak will prove too sectiony. Usually smaller than the prevailing swell.

**The Environment.** Walk over the hill from El Remanso. Banditos work this area at dusk — don't take any valuables or leave stuff on the beach.

### 10. El Yanke
**URL :** https://stormrider.surf/break/el-yanke

**The Surf.** El Yanke is a good beachbreak with well-defined peaks, down some ropey dirt roads. Even harder to get to are the better high-tide peaks found two beaches north at Playa Hermosa, where all abilities will find something to their liking without the crowd.

**The Environment.** Needs more swell to break, but is ideal for improvers.

### 11. El Coco
**URL :** https://stormrider.surf/break/el-coco

**The Surf.** El Coco isn't as good quality waves as Hermosa or Yanke, but the main road hits the coast making access easy, and it's always a bit smaller and more manageable than breaks to the north, not to mention way less crowded.

**The Environment.** Another good learners beach and the wildlife refuge makes a beautiful backdrop.

### 12. Sally Ann's
**URL :** https://stormrider.surf/break/sally-anns

**The Surf.** Beyond the turtle nesting sites along the beach of Playa la Flor, the long, hollow left point just south is known as Sally Ann's and should only be attempted on huge swells, because when it's under double overhead, rocks litter this break.

**The Environment.** Rangers often chase surfers away during nesting season.

## REGION 2 — CENTRAL NICARAGUA (17 spots)

### 13. Hemorrhoids
**URL :** https://stormrider.surf/break/hemorrhoids

**The Surf.** The left reef at Hemorrhoids prefers more water over the rocks and can have some rapid-fire walls and barrels for the guaranteed crowd that come from the resort and beyond. There's a few more high-tide reefs heading south round to San Diego.

**The Environment.** Suffers from dubious water quality and a bit of attitude in the water.

### 14. Playa Ausuchillas
**URL :** https://stormrider.surf/break/playa-ausuchillas

**The Surf.** Playa Ausuchillas consistently handles swells of most sizes and is a go-to for small peaky days when there will be some nice peelers for all abilities. Gets demanding at size, with big challenging barrels. Likes low tide.

**The Environment.** Pay entrance fee to enter Gran Pacifica Resort which can provide a semi crowd along with the local posse, but there's lots of room up the beach.

### 15. Chiggas
**URL :** https://stormrider.surf/break/chiggas

**The Surf.** More high-tide reefs hug the rocks north of Playa San Diego, an exposed stretch of black sand beach for all abilities on small days. Chiggas is popular because it handles any size and is an easier deepwater roll-in to barrel and turn sections.

**The Environment.** In front of the golf course and rivermouth which may bring pollution after rains.

### 16. El Transito
**URL :** https://stormrider.surf/break/el-transito

**The Surf.** El Transito vacuums up all available swell onto constantly moving sandbars, shaped by refraction off the reef protrusions at either end. Fun waves for all until it gets overhead and quickly closes-out.

**The Environment.** Could be a crowd from the surf camps up north on the small days. Beware the scattered patches of reef.

### 17. Montelimar
**URL :** https://stormrider.surf/break/montelimar

**The Surf.** The private, all-inclusive beach resort of Montelimar fronts an average beachbreak with a sluggish left sometimes forming off the southern point. Best ridden on a small peaky swell and pushing tide, when the odd inside tube section can spice up a session.

**The Environment.** Rarely a crowd because it's rarely worth surfing.

### 18. Masachapa
**URL :** https://stormrider.surf/break/masachapa

**The Surf.** Masachapa's rights will only go off on a rare combination of big W swell and low tide on a windless day since NE is sideshore. Also check out the nearby pier on the protected beach.

**The Environment.** Rarely breaks, so rarely ridden.

### 19. Pochomil
**URL :** https://stormrider.surf/break/pochomil

**The Surf.** Pochomil is a popular vacation spot only 1h drive from Managua. It picks up plenty of S swell and there are 3 rivermouths to help sculpt sandbars. Check for a slow, rolling A-frame down Pochomil Viejo.

**The Environment.** Lots of reefy patches but still mainly sand-dependent. Gets crowded with swimmers and some surfers during holidays.

### 20. Shacks – Miramar
**URL :** https://stormrider.surf/break/shacks-miramar

**The Surf.** Flatter slab-like reef that still requires sand, swell and tide to be right for some fun inviting barrels and walls for blasting. The lefts are always better and it needs moderate swells to work.

**The Environment.** Doesn't handle a crowd so when it works, it suddenly fills up in the short tide window.

### 21. Punta Miramar 🔒 (Paywalled)
**URL :** https://stormrider.surf/break/punta-miramar

Full content requires Stormrider Passport. Inferred from neighbouring spots' text: holds consistently good lefts, with fast inside runners and tubes on the inside section.

### 22. Pipes – Miramar
**URL :** https://stormrider.surf/break/pipes-miramar

**The Surf.** Pipes is the rocky beachbreak barrels to the north of Miramar Point in small to medium swells. Prefers low tide up to overhead S-W swells and serves up some decent barrels and performance walls.

**The Environment.** Not really a great beginner spot as it can be hollow and punchy over a rocky bottom in places.

### 23. Puerto Sandino
**URL :** https://stormrider.surf/break/puerto-sandino

**The Surf.** Touted as one of Nicaragua's best waves and could also be the longest, with rides up to 400m possible when the reef-anchored rivermouth sands pile up in the perfect triangle. Take-offs can be fast and vertical straight into the first of multiple barrel sections, broken up by lip-smacking walls. Likes a higher, pushing tide when the outside peaks soften and the NE winds are dead offshore. Most local surf camps access via boat, otherwise it's a long, current-swept paddle.

**The Environment.** Not for beginners unless it's small and the inside breaks softly. Highly consistent with regular crowds and a bit of vibe on occasion between visitors and ex-pats.

### 24. Casares
**URL :** https://stormrider.surf/break/casares

**The Surf.** Casares needs a good swell to get through the offshore reefs and focus in on the rocky shoreline where several peaks will work, usually at higher tides. Fickle and hard to read.

**The Environment.** Not good for beginners at this small fishing village with friendly locals.

### 25. Salinas Grandes
**URL :** https://stormrider.surf/break/salinas-grandes

**The Surf.** Open beachbreak best with peaky W swell and plenty of peaks to choose from all the way up to the rivermouth.

**The Environment.** Never a crowd and good for beginners when small, otherwise there will be strong currents.

### 26. Playgrounds – Veracruz de Acayo
**URL :** https://stormrider.surf/break/playgrounds-veracruz-de-acayo

**The Surf.** Boat in to remote Playgrounds, where sweet, inviting lefts and shorter rights scurry across the barnacle-encrusted cobbles, offering fun skatepark walls in moderate swells from the S-SW. Predictable, ripable and good for intermediates, but avoid touching the sharp bottom.

**The Environment.** Long boat trip from Popoyo, but there can still be a crowd sometimes.

### 27. Lance's Left – El Astillero
**URL :** https://stormrider.surf/break/lances-left-el-astillero

**The Surf.** Surprisingly long lefthand point that offers easy rollers and shoulders in most swell directions and sizes. Best accessed by boat on a dropping tide for fun rides for most abilities unless big.

**The Environment.** Watch out for urchins and petty theft from cars and beach.

### 28. El Astillero
**URL :** https://stormrider.surf/break/el-astillero

**The Surf.** El Astillero gets great beachbreaks, particularly up by the rivermouth where the rights get hollow and pack plenty of punch. The protected south end of the beach is usually a lot smaller.

**The Environment.** Lots of rocks in the line-up past the rivermouth. Petty crime an issue if you leave a vehicle here and it's a long walk from town.

### 29. Poneloya
**URL :** https://stormrider.surf/break/poneloya

**The Surf.** Poneloya and Las Penitas have a bit more shape to their beachbreaks than the long straight sands to the north and south. Doesn't pick up as much swell as down south but when it does, it quickly gets heavy rips and close-outs.

**The Environment.** Empty and rarely surfed despite Quiksilver holding a comp here.
